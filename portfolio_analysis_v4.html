8Macro Context: Why Your Portfolio Looks Like This in 2025–2026

The 2024–2025 Tech Bubble Deflation

By late 2024, US mega-cap tech (Nvidia, Apple, Microsoft, Tesla) had become extremely expensive, with valuations stretching far beyond historical norms. Earnings growth couldn't keep pace. This created a valuation reset in 2025, during which Nvidia fell from $130 to $98, and Tesla declined further. Your decision to sell Degiro holdings at these prices and rotate into European "boring" stocks reflects a rational timing call: you got out near the peak.

The European Value Rotation

Simultaneously, European equities (especially French luxury and industrial) became cheap relative to their historical valuations and US alternatives. Stocks like Vinci, Eiffage, LVMH, Hermès offered dividend yields, lower growth expectations (= less downside risk), and political/economic stability in the EU. Your portfolio reflects this rotation into the "boring but safe" category of equities.

Crypto Cycle Bottom (2024–2026)

After the 2021 peak and 2022 collapse, crypto (Bitcoin and Ethereum) bottomed in late 2024 and began a recovery. You entered Bitcoin and Ethereum in late 2025 / early 2026 as position-sizing bets on a potential multi-year bull cycle. Down 10–15% as of March 2026, they're still early in their holding period.

Interest Rates & JPY Carry Trade

Your margin position in Japanese yen to buy Shiseido reflects an implicit carry trade view: you're borrowing cheap JPY (at near-zero rates in Japan) to invest in an equity. As long as JPY stays weak and Shiseido appreciates, this works. However, JPY has trended stronger since late 2024, which is a headwind. Your Shiseido position is up 39% in JPY terms but closer to flat in EUR terms after FX adjustment—illustrating the FX headwind.
